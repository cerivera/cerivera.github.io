Quick Tips for Speaking Catalan

  • The Catalan "r" is lightly trilled, similar to Spanish. At the end of a word it's usually silent.
  • Unstressed "a" and "e" both sound like a short "uh" (schwa) — similar to the "a" in "about."
  • The letters "tx" make a "ch" sound, and "ig" at the end of a word sounds like "tch."
  • Catalan "j" and "g" (before e/i) sound like the "zh" in "measure," not like English "j."
  • People deeply appreciate when you try to speak Catalan instead of defaulting to Spanish — even a simple bon dia goes a long way.
  • The emergency number across the EU is 112. It works from any phone.
